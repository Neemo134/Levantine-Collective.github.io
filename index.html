About The Levantine Collective and Aya Tarabeine

The Levantine Collective: Reclaiming Identity Through Art, Story, and Community "The Levantine Collective is a creative and cultural community that celebrates the people, stories, and heritage of the Levant through art, conversation, and collaboration," explains founder Aya Tarabeine. Based in London, Aya has built a sanctuary for Levantine identity, rooted in storytelling, art, and connection. Born in Syria and later displaced to the UAE before settling in the UK, Aya’s journey is one of navigating belonging and complexity. “We wanted to build something that brings people together and lets them tell their own stories, their own way.”

Philosophy and Values of the Community

To her, "Levantine isn’t a rigid identity; it’s an anchor point." The Collective embraces this expansiveness — from queer and diasporic experiences to radical and evolving expressions of self. Their work, from exhibitions to intimate events, celebrates the richness and contradictions of the Levant. Rather than chasing clicks or clout, success for them is human: "If our work sparks conversation and connection, we’re doing something right." With a focus on community, care, and courageous creativity, The Levantine Collective is a space where people can feel both rooted and free. It doesn’t flatten identity — it opens it up.
